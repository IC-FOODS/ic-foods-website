# Transaction and Traceability Guidelines

Comprehensive transaction records and traceability systems enable supply chain transparency and support food safety initiatives. This guideline defines requirements for transaction documentation and traceability.

## Transaction Records

All transactions must include:

- **Transaction ID**: Unique identifier for the transaction
- **Transaction Date**: Date and time of transaction
- **Buyer Information**: Buyer name and identifier
- **Seller Information**: Seller name and identifier
- **Product Details**: Product name, quantity, unit price, total price
- **Lot/Batch Information**: Lot code or batch identifier

## Traceability Requirements

### One Step Forward, One Step Back

Each transaction must enable:

- **Forward Traceability**: Identify where products were sent
- **Backward Traceability**: Identify source of products

### Required Data Elements

- **Origin**: Farm or production facility identifier
- **Processing**: Processing facility identifiers (if applicable)
- **Distribution**: Distribution center or warehouse identifiers
- **Destination**: Final destination or customer identifier

## Digital Records

All transaction records must be:

- Stored in digital format
- Accessible for at least 2 years
- Searchable by transaction ID, date, or product
- Exportable in standard formats (CSV, JSON)

## Lot Tracking

Link all transactions to lot codes to enable:

- Rapid recall capabilities
- Quality tracking
- Supply chain analysis

## Compliance

Transaction systems must comply with:

- Local food safety regulations
- International trade requirements
- Data privacy regulations
