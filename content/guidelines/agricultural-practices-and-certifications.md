# Agricultural Practices and Certifications

Documentation of agricultural practices and certifications provides transparency and enables informed purchasing decisions. This guideline outlines required documentation for farming practices and certification standards.

## Required Documentation

All produce must include documentation for:

- **Growing Method**: Conventional, organic, integrated pest management (IPM)
- **Certification Status**: Organic, Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance, etc.
- **Certification Body**: Issuing organization and certification number
- **Harvest Date**: Date of harvest or harvest window
- **Farm Location**: Geographic coordinates or farm identifier

## Certification Standards

### Organic Certification

Organic certification must be verified through recognized certifying bodies. Include:

- Certification number
- Certifying organization
- Certification validity period
- Scope of certification (crop, farm, or operation)

### Other Certifications

Document any additional certifications such as:

- Fair Trade
- Rainforest Alliance
- GlobalG.A.P.
- Food safety certifications (HACCP, ISO 22000)

## Practice Documentation

### Pesticide Use

Document pesticide application history including:

- Active ingredients used
- Application dates
- Pre-harvest intervals observed
- Compliance with maximum residue limits (MRLs)

### Water Management

Record water source and irrigation practices:

- Water source type (surface, groundwater, reclaimed)
- Irrigation method
- Water quality testing results

## Data Requirements

All practice and certification data must be:

- Verifiable through third-party audits or documentation
- Accessible in digital format
- Updated within 30 days of certification changes
